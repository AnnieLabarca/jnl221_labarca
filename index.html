The Largest Vocabulary in Hip Hop

The project I chose was “The Largest Vocabulary in Hip Hop” by Matt Daniels. The project focused on the number of unique words rappers have used in their lyrics. It was recently updated in 2019 to reflect some of the newer, bigger rappers. The first 35,000 lyrics of a rapper’s songs were considered, and those that did not have the necessary amount were unfortunately left out. The lyrics were studied and reviewed on Genius, a media annotation site. The biggest problem with this project was the lack of a clear definition of what a “unique” word was (the grammar was discussed, but not the meaning). The project has three major dataviz elements that are visually appealing to the eye, showing ranges of unique words by decades, how the data compares to other genres and a lineup of individual artists. All internalize the fact that hip hop has changed and evolved in the way that old school rappers became famous for certain bars while newer rappers can use the production of their song to reel more fans in. This project is notable because Daniels gives enough sufficient reading material and background to go with the dataviz parts without it being overwhelming. Two of the three dataviz elements are color coded, making it completely clear what information is being shared. This project could be considered visceral in the sense that it caters to an audience that is nostalgic towards rap as a genre. When comparing the old sound to the new, there is a kind of fondness towards the way songwriting used to be, as some people today would argue that there was more creativity. Even put more simply, it’s fun to potentially see your favorite artist represented at large. Music as a whole is visceral, as it evokes certain feelings and memories right away. Daniels successfully combined those feelings with an entertaining display, which is why it was noteworthy.
